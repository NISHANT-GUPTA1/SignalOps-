# AI Bug Triage
### Turn messy bug reports into clean, prioritized issues — automatically

---

## The problem we solve

Bug reports arrive as a mess. A vague Slack message here, a half-written email there, a duplicate of something filed last week. Before an engineer can even start, someone has to rewrite it, judge how urgent it is, check whether it's already been reported, and file it properly.

The result: **hours of triage busywork, duplicate issues everywhere, and real bugs buried under noise.**

**AI Bug Triage does the triage for you — and grounds every decision in your team's actual history.**

---

## What it does for you

- **Turns raw reports into proper issues** — a clear title, a clean summary, reproduction steps, expected vs. actual behavior — from whatever messy input comes in.
- **Prioritizes automatically** — assigns urgency and severity with a clear reason, so the important bugs rise to the top.
- **Catches duplicates before they're filed** — checks every new report against your existing issues, so you don't fix the same thing twice.
- **Files it where you work** — creates the issue directly in GitHub or your project tracker with one click.
- **Writes your release notes** — turns resolved issues into a clean, ready-to-share changelog.
- **Grounded in your real history** — every decision is based on your team's own past issues, not a generic guess.

---

## The value it adds

| Before | After |
|---|---|
| Every bug report is triaged by hand | Reports arrive already triaged and prioritized |
| Duplicates get filed and worked twice | Duplicates caught before they waste anyone's time |
| Priority is a debate | Priority is assigned, explained, and consistent |
| Release notes are a manual scramble | Release notes generate themselves |

**Bottom line:** engineers spend their time fixing bugs instead of sorting them — and nothing important gets lost in the pile.

Engineering and support teams at SaaS and tech companies drowning in incoming bug reports and manual triage.
